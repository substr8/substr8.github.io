Summary

During the academic school year 2020-2021 and 2021-2022, I taught the subject ITBA 121 - IT for Business Analytics at Saint Louis University. The course was an in-depth study of spreadsheets and their application in Business Analytics. Completing this course enabled students to master advanced formulas and functions of spreadsheet applications, produce more sophisticated reports, and perform complex mathematical and statistical calculations, thus saving time and improving productivity in completing business tasks. This course sets out essential concepts and skills relating to understanding the concept of spreadsheets and demonstrating an ability to use a spreadsheet to produce accurate work outputs and provide insights for business decision-making.

The following are some lecture videos/tutorials I did for the said course:

1. Excel Conditional Functions Playlist

Click on the video to start watching the playlist. Topics discussed include the IF function, nested IF functions, COUNTIF, SUMIF, and conditional formatting.

2. Excel Conditional Functions Playlist

Lookup functions in Excel mean referencing a cell to match values in another row or column against the cell, thereby retrieving the corresponding results from the respective rows and columns. Topics discussed include VLOOKUP, HLOOKUP, and the INDEX MATCH combo functions.

3. Pivot Tables

If you are working with large data sets in Excel, pivot table comes in really handy as a quick way to make an interactive summary from many records. Among other things, it can automatically sort and filter different subsets of data, count totals, calculate average as well as create cross tabulations. Another benefit of using pivot tables is that you can set up and change the structure of your summary table simply by dragging and dropping the source table's columns. This rotation or pivoting gave the feature its name.

4. The ABC Global Case Study

I created the above case study as a review for my students for their final exam for the subject. It incorporates Pivot Tables and Dashboard Creation.

5. Data Analysis Toolpak

If you need to develop complex statistical or engineering analyses, you can save steps and time by using the Analysis ToolPak. You provide the data and parameters for each analysis, and the tool uses the appropriate statistical or engineering macro functions to calculate and display the results in an output table. Some tools generate charts in addition to output tables.
