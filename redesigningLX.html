Summary

I was tasked to think back and reflect on the worst learning experience (LX) I've ever had if I was given a chance to redesign that LX to make it better.

It's your chance to be a learning experience designer! Try to think about what made this LX such a bad experience, and how it could be improved. What were the original goals of this LX? Why did it fail and how could it reach them? - Eskwelabs

Video Discussion


It was tough to pinpoint a specific LX. So what I did was generalize my two-year teaching data analytics during the pandemic. It became a retrospect of what I can improve as a teacher and how I design course syllabi and other instructional materials. The problem I focused on was "How can we engage and activate students for the entire semester?" regardless of teaching modality.
